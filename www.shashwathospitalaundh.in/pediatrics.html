Pediatrics

Pediatric care is a branch of medicine that involves the medical care of infants, children, and adolescents. It focuses on the physical, emotional, and social health of children from birth to young adulthood. Pediatricians work to ensure that children achieve their full potential for health and well-being.

Hernia

A hernia occurs when an internal organ or tissue pushes through a weak spot in the abdominal wall. Surgery is typically required to repair the hernia.

Hydrocele

A hydrocele is a fluid-filled sac around a testicle. It may cause swelling in the scrotum and may require surgery if it persists or causes discomfort.

Appendix (Appendicitis)

Appendicitis is an inflammation of the appendix that often requires removal through an appendectomy to prevent complications.

Intussusception

Intussusception is when a part of the intestine folds into itself, leading to obstruction. Treatment usually involves surgery or sometimes an air contrast enema.

Phimosis

Phimosis is a condition where the foreskin of the penis cannot be retracted over the glans. Treatment options include topical steroid creams or circumcision if it causes problems.

Hypospadias

Hypospadias is a congenital condition where the urethra opens on the underside of the penis. Surgical correction is usually performed before the child is 18 months old.
