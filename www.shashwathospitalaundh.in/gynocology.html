Gynocology

Gynecological surgery involves various procedures to address conditions affecting the female reproductive system. These surgeries range from removing parts of the reproductive organs to correcting structural issues and managing conditions like stress incontinence. Here’s an overview of some common gynecological surgeries:

Gynecological Surgery

Gynecological surgery addresses various conditions affecting the female reproductive system. Here are some common procedures:

Total Hysterectomy ± Bilateral/Unilateral Salpingo-Oophorectomy

Total hysterectomy involves the removal of the uterus, and it may include the removal of one or both ovaries and fallopian tubes, depending on the condition.

Subtotal Hysterectomy

Subtotal hysterectomy removes the uterus but leaves the cervix intact. It is often performed when the cervix does not have cancer or other significant conditions.

Myomectomy

Myomectomy is the surgical removal of fibroids (myomas) from the uterus, aimed at preserving the uterus for women who wish to maintain fertility.

Ovarian Cystectomy

Ovarian cystectomy involves the removal of cysts from the ovaries. This procedure is performed to alleviate pain or prevent complications caused by cysts.

Oophorectomy/Salpingo-Oophorectomy

Oophorectomy involves the removal of one or both ovaries. Salpingo-oophorectomy also includes the removal of the fallopian tubes along with the ovaries.

Salpingectomy

Salpingectomy is the removal of one or both fallopian tubes, often performed to treat conditions such as ectopic pregnancy or certain types of infection.

Surgery for Stress Incontinence

Surgery for stress incontinence aims to correct the weakness in the pelvic floor that causes involuntary leakage of urine during physical activities like coughing or exercise.

Adhesiolysis/Tuboplasty

Adhesiolysis is the surgical removal of adhesions that cause pain or organ dysfunction. Tuboplasty is performed to reconstruct damaged fallopian tubes to restore fertility.

Wertheim's Hysterectomy

Wertheim's hysterectomy is an extensive procedure for treating cervical cancer, involving the removal of the uterus, cervix, surrounding tissues, and often, nearby lymph nodes.
