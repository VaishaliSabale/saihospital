Laparoscopic Surgery

Laparoscopic surgery, also known as minimally invasive surgery or keyhole surgery, is a type of surgical procedure performed through small incisions using a laparoscope—a long, thin tube with a high-intensity light and a high-resolution camera at the front.

VIEW MORE

Endoscopy

Endoscopy is a medical procedure that allows doctors to view the inside of a person's body using an instrument called an endoscope. This procedure can be both diagnostic and therapeutic, meaning it can be used to identify problems and to perform certain treatments..

VIEW MORE

Laser Proctology

Laser proctology refers to the use of laser technology to treat conditions related to the rectum and anus. This modern technique is utilized for various proctological issues, and quicker recovery times compared to traditional surgical methods.

VIEW MORE

Urology

Urology is a branch of medicine that focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of diseases and conditions related to the urinary tract and the male reproductive system.

VIEW MORE

Plastic Surgery

Plastic surgery is a medical specialty focused on the correction, reconstruction, or enhancement of the body. This can include cosmetic (aesthetic) procedures aimed at improving appearance and reconstructive procedures aimed at restoring function and normal appearance after injury, disease, or congenital defects.

VIEW MORE

Canser Surgery

Cancer surgery, also known as oncologic surgery, involves the surgical removal of cancerous tumors and surrounding tissues to prevent the spread of cancer within the body.

VIEW MORE

Gynocology

Gynecology is a branch of medicine that specializes in the health of the female reproductive systems (vagina, uterus, and ovaries) and the breasts. Gynecologists are medical doctors who focus on diagnosing, treating, and managing diseases and conditions specific to women.

VIEW MORE

E N T

ENT stands for Ear, Nose, and Throat, which refers to the medical specialty focused on the diagnosis and treatment of disorders related to the ear, nose, throat, head, and neck.
