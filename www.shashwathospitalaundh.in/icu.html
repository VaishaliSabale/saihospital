ICU

An Intensive Care Unit (ICU) is a special department of a hospital or health care facility that provides intensive treatment medicine. ICUs cater to patients with severe or life-threatening illnesses and injuries that require constant care, close monitoring, and support from specialist equipment and medications.

What is ICU?

An Intensive Care Unit (ICU) is a specialized section of a hospital dedicated to providing intensive treatment and monitoring for patients with severe or life-threatening conditions. ICUs are equipped with advanced medical technology and staffed by highly trained healthcare professionals.

Key Features of an ICU:

  • Advanced Monitoring: Continuous monitoring of vital signs such as heart rate, blood pressure, and oxygen levels.
  • Specialized Equipment: Use of ventilators, dialysis machines, and other life-support devices.
  • Highly Skilled Staff: A team of specialized healthcare professionals, including critical care physicians, nurses, respiratory therapists, and other specialists.
  • Comprehensive Care: Management of a wide range of critical conditions such as severe infections, trauma, heart attacks, and post-surgical care.
