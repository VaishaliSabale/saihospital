Leparoscopic Surgery

Laparoscopic surgery, also known as minimally invasive surgery, is a modern surgical technique in which operations are performed through small incisions using a camera and specialized instruments. This approach offers several benefits, including reduced pain, shorter recovery time, and smaller scars compared to traditional open surgery.

Laparoscopic Surgery

Laparoscopic surgery is a minimally invasive procedure used to perform operations through small incisions with the aid of a camera. This technique is commonly used for various conditions, providing benefits such as reduced pain, shorter recovery time, and smaller scars.

Appendix (Appendectomy)

Laparoscopic appendectomy is the removal of the appendix through small incisions, often performed to treat appendicitis.

Gall Bladder (Cholecystectomy)

Laparoscopic cholecystectomy is the removal of the gallbladder, commonly due to gallstones causing pain or infection.

Hernia Repair

Laparoscopic hernia repair involves correcting hernias by placing a mesh through small incisions to reinforce the abdominal wall.

Ovarian Cyst Removal

Laparoscopic surgery to remove ovarian cysts involves excising the cysts while preserving the ovary, minimizing recovery time.

Bowel Surgery

Laparoscopic bowel surgery involves procedures such as resection or removal of parts of the bowel for conditions like cancer or obstruction.
