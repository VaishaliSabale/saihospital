General Surgery

General surgery encompasses a wide range of procedures. Here, we'll discuss common operations for various conditions, including: Appendix (Appendectomy), Gallbladder stones (Cholelithiasis), Hernia, Hydrocele, Piles, fissure, fistula, Intestinal obstruction, Colonic obstruction, Cancer surgery for the bowel.

General Surgery Operations

Appendectomy

Appendectomy is the surgical removal of the appendix, often performed to treat appendicitis.

Cholecystectomy

Cholecystectomy is the removal of the gallbladder, commonly due to gallstones (cholelithiasis).

Hernia Repair

Hernia repair involves the surgical correction of hernias, which occur when an organ pushes through an opening in the muscle or tissue.

Hydrocele Repair

Hydrocele repair addresses the accumulation of fluid around a testicle, typically requiring surgical intervention.

Piles, Fissure, Fistula

Treatment for piles (hemorrhoids), fissures, and fistulas often involves surgical procedures to alleviate pain and discomfort.

Intestinal Obstruction

Surgery for intestinal obstruction aims to remove blockages that prevent the passage of contents through the intestines.

Colonic Obstruction

Colonic obstruction surgery addresses blockages in the colon, ensuring normal bowel function.

Cancer Surgery for Bowel

Cancer surgery for the bowel involves removing cancerous growths to treat and prevent the spread of cancer.
