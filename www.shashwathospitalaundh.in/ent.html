ENT (Ear, Nose, and Throat) Surgery

ENT surgery includes a range of procedures to treat conditions affecting the ear, nose, and throat. Here are some common surgeries:

Tonsillectomy

Tonsillectomy involves the removal of the tonsils, often performed to treat recurrent tonsillitis or sleep apnea.

Adenoidectomy

Adenoidectomy is the surgical removal of the adenoids, typically done to address chronic infections or breathing difficulties in children.

Foreign Body Removal (Nose)

This procedure involves the removal of foreign objects lodged in the nasal passages, which can cause obstruction or irritation.

Rhinoplasty (Deviated Nasal Septum)

Rhinoplasty can correct a deviated nasal septum, improving airflow and aesthetic appearance by reshaping the nose and septum.

Tracheostomy

Tracheostomy involves creating an opening in the trachea (windpipe) to facilitate breathing when the upper airway is obstructed or compromised.
