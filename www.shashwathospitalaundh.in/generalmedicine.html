General Medicine

General medicine, also known as internal medicine, is a medical specialty dealing with the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of adult diseases. Physicians specializing in general medicine are called internists. They manage patients with complex, chronic illnesses and provide comprehensive care.

General Medicine

General medicine involves the diagnosis and treatment of a wide range of diseases and conditions affecting various systems of the body. Below are some common conditions managed under general medicine.

Typhoid Fever

A bacterial infection caused by Salmonella typhi, characterized by high fever, weakness, and abdominal pain.

Dengue Fever

A viral infection transmitted by mosquitoes, presenting with high fever, severe headache, pain behind the eyes, joint and muscle pain.

Malaria

A mosquito-borne infectious disease caused by Plasmodium parasites, leading to symptoms like fever, chills, and flu-like illness.

Viral Fever

Fever resulting from a viral infection, often accompanied by fatigue, body aches, and headaches.

Cough

A reflex action to clear the airways of mucus, irritants, or foreign particles.

Bronchitis

Inflammation of the bronchial tubes, leading to cough, mucus production, and difficulty breathing.

Pancreatitis

Inflammation of the pancreas, causing abdominal pain, nausea, and vomiting.

Cirrhosis

Chronic liver damage from various causes leading to scarring and liver failure.

Jaundice

A condition characterized by yellowing of the skin and eyes due to elevated bilirubin levels.

Gastroenterology

The branch of medicine focused on the digestive system and its disorders.

Arthritis

Inflammation of the joints, causing pain, swelling, and reduced motion.

Bronchial Asthma

A chronic inflammatory disease of the airways, causing wheezing, shortness of breath, and coughing.
