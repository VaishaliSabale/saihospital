Urological Surgery

Urological surgery includes a range of procedures to treat conditions of the urinary tract and male reproductive system. Here are some common surgeries:

PCNL (Percutaneous Nephrolithotomy) for Kidney Stones

PCNL is a minimally invasive procedure used to remove large kidney stones through a small incision in the back, guided by imaging techniques.

Nephrolithotomy for Kidney Stones

Nephrolithotomy involves surgically removing kidney stones through an incision in the kidney, typically used for stones that are too large to be treated with other methods.

Lithotripsy for Ureteric Stones (URS)

Lithotripsy uses shock waves to break ureteric stones into smaller pieces, which can then be passed naturally. This procedure is non-invasive and typically performed with the help of endoscopic techniques.

Nephrectomy for Non-Functioning Kidney

Nephrectomy involves the surgical removal of a non-functioning or diseased kidney, often performed when the kidney is severely damaged or poses a risk to overall health.

Bladder Stones Operation

This procedure involves the removal of stones from the bladder, which can cause pain, urinary obstruction, and infections. Techniques may include cystoscopy and laser lithotripsy.

Ureteric Reimplantation

Ureteric reimplantation is a surgical procedure to reposition and reattach the ureter to the bladder, often performed to correct issues such as ureteral reflux or obstruction.
