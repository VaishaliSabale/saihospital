Orthopaedic Surgery

Orthopaedic surgery encompasses a variety of procedures aimed at treating musculoskeletal issues. Here are some common orthopaedic surgeries:

Fracture Repair

Fracture repair involves stabilizing broken bones to allow them to heal properly. This can be done using methods such as casting, internal fixation with plates and screws, or external fixation.

Implant Surgery

Implant surgery involves placing artificial devices, such as joint replacements or spinal implants, to restore function or alleviate pain caused by joint degeneration or spinal issues.

Arthroscopy

Arthroscopy is a minimally invasive procedure used to diagnose and treat joint problems. It involves inserting a small camera and instruments through tiny incisions to view and repair damaged tissues inside the joint.

Knee Injury Treatment

Treatment for knee injuries may include surgical interventions such as meniscus repair, ligament reconstruction, or patellar realignment to address issues like tears, sprains, or dislocations.

C-Arm Facility

The C-arm facility provides real-time imaging during surgical procedures. This mobile X-ray machine helps guide orthopaedic surgeons in accurately placing implants and assessing alignment.
