Endoscopy

Endoscopy is a medical procedure that allows doctors to examine the interior surfaces of an organ or tissue. It is commonly used to investigate symptoms such as pain, swallowing difficulties, nausea, and vomiting. It can also help in diagnosing conditions like severe anemia, foreign bodies in the gastrointestinal tract (GIT), and hoarseness of voice.

Endoscopy

Endoscopy is a medical procedure used to examine the interior surfaces of an organ or tissue. It helps in diagnosing and managing various conditions, providing a clear view for accurate treatment.

Epigastric & Retrosternal Pain

Endoscopy can help identify the cause of upper abdominal and chest pain, such as ulcers or gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD).

Dysphagia & Odynophagia

Difficulty or pain in swallowing can be investigated using endoscopy to detect obstructions, strictures, or inflammation in the esophagus.

Nausea & Vomiting

Persistent nausea and vomiting may require endoscopy to determine the underlying cause, such as gastric ulcers or blockages.

Hiccough

Endoscopy can help identify any gastrointestinal issues causing chronic hiccups, ensuring proper management.

Hematemesis & Melena

Endoscopy is crucial for diagnosing and treating upper gastrointestinal bleeding, presenting as vomiting blood or black stools.

Severe Anemia & Loss of Appetite

Endoscopy can evaluate severe anemia and loss of appetite to detect any gastrointestinal bleeding or malignancies.

Foreign Body in GIT

Endoscopy is an effective method for locating and removing foreign bodies ingested into the gastrointestinal tract.

Hoarseness of Voice

Endoscopy can help diagnose the cause of hoarseness, such as laryngopharyngeal reflux or vocal cord issues.
