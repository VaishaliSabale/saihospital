Oncosurgery / Cancer Surgery

Cancer surgery involves the removal of tumors and surrounding tissues to treat various types of cancer. Here are some common surgeries:

Thyroid Cancer

Thyroid cancer surgery often involves the removal of all or part of the thyroid gland. This may include a total thyroidectomy or partial thyroidectomy, depending on the extent of the cancer.

Breast Cancer

Surgical options for breast cancer include lumpectomy (removal of the tumor and a small margin of surrounding tissue) and mastectomy (removal of one or both breasts), based on the stage and type of cancer.

Stomach Cancer

Stomach cancer surgery may involve partial or total gastrectomy, where part or all of the stomach is removed. This procedure is often combined with other treatments such as chemotherapy or radiation.

Small Bowel Cancer

Surgery for small bowel cancer typically involves the removal of the affected segment of the small intestine. This may also include resection of nearby lymph nodes if the cancer has spread.

Colon Cancer

Colon cancer surgery involves removing the affected portion of the colon along with nearby lymph nodes. This can be done through traditional open surgery or minimally invasive laparoscopic techniques.
